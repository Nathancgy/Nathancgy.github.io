Passions

I really enjoy computer science and love taking part in competitions like Usaco and Codeforces, where I use C++. I also like working on open source projects, especially making websites (like this one!) and working on Python projects. Apart from these, I've done a few research projects too. When I'm not doing STEM stuff, my favorite hobbies are reading, playing basketball, and snowboarding.

Past Journey

It all began with the simple joy of creation, piecing together LEGO blocks, which soon transformed into the digital crafting of structures and robots within the pixelated realms of Minecraft. This virtual playground was where I first encountered the magic of programming. I was drawn to the logic and creativity it offered, leading me to transition from the intuitive drag-and-drop environment of Scratch to the more structured syntax of languages. As my curiosity grew, so did my ambition to delve deeper, which brought me to C++ through the challenges of Usaco, Luogu, and the enthusiastic spirit of Codeforces.
My journey took a collaborative turn when I discovered GitHub while self learning algorithms on Usaco guide, which is an open source website. Github opened my eyes to the vast potential of open source work. Here, I found a community where I could contribute, learn, and grow alongside programmers from all corners of the globe. Moving forward, I hope to leverage these experiences to contribute to meaningful projects that can make a positive impact on our world.
